## **Using AI Tools to Build Offseason Plans for Players**

  

I started with the Player Evaluation Tool.

Each player who came to me and said, "Coach, I want to get better," got more than just a pat on the back. I used their stats, coach notes, and performance summaries to create a personalized workout plan they could do at home, at a park, or with a teammate.

We didn’t need a gym — just cones, chairs, or chalk lines. The goal was to make the workouts real and usable.

The AI generated workouts tailored to what they needed most. Footwork, shooting, agility, confidence-building — based on actual gaps in their game.

This tool helped bridge the gap between the end of the season and day one of the next.

  

  

## **AI Team Evaluation for Next Season Strategy**

  

The Team Evaluation Tool helped me zoom out.

I put in our final season stats, removed graduating seniors, and added my returning players. For a few incoming JV and freshmen players, I gave short notes — enough to keep them on the radar, but not overhype them.

From there, I got a full scouting report of my _next year’s team_. It gave me:- The type of offense we’re best built for- Lineup and positional suggestions- Key team weaknesses to work on this summer- A starting point for next season’s philosophy

Then I took those insights and pushed them into other tools I use — the AI Assistant for messaging and prep, and the Practice Planner for designing workouts that target our issues head-on.

  

  

## **This Is What AI Should Do for Coaches**

  

It’s not about replacing your instincts. It’s about giving you clarity and saving you time.

I’m not guessing what to tell a kid who wants to get better — I’ve got a tool that gives them a real plan. I’m not waiting until next year to think about who’s filling what role — I’ve got a full-season outlook built from my own data.
