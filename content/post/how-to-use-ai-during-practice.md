### 🧠 **What AI Can Do During Practice**

###   

Re-sequence your plan mid-session

If your original plan falls apart, the Practice Planner (Lite or Full) rebuilds a custom version instantly based on who's present and what needs work.

  

Make lineup or grouping decisions

The AI Coaching Assistant helps with logic-based grouping, subbing options, or how to keep things competitive with reduced numbers.

  

Handle player focus and motivation

Ask: “What’s the best way to refocus a team that’s distracted or low-energy?”

The Assistant gives quick, coach-friendly suggestions.

  

Redirect youth teams quickly

While the Assistant doesn’t generate drills, it can give structure or game ideas for 8U–10U athletes to stay engaged.

  

\---

  

### 📱 **How I Actually Use AI During Practice**

  

Saved PDFs on my phone

I upload the tool into ChatGPT or Claude and type my question.

“Only 5 players today. What should I focus on?”

  

Use voice-to-text

When I don’t want to type, I dictate the question and get a clear, fast answer.

  

Label my AI chat folders

I keep chats like “Small Group Adjustments” or “Post-Game Plan Ideas” saved for reuse.

### 🌞 **BONUS: When Only 4 Girls Show Up in the Summer**

###   

Summer practice problems are real. Between camps, vacations, and jobs, you show up and see four athletes.

  

Here’s how I handle it:

  

Rebuild with the Planner

I input: “4 players. Focus on ball handling, shooting, 2v2 reads.”

It gives me a clear, efficient plan.

  

Use the AI Assistant for format tips

I ask: “What are good ways to keep 4 players engaged in a competitive summer session?”

It returns ideas for ladders, rotations, energy resets, and rewards.

Make it a bonus day

The Assistant helps me reframe practice as a win for the kids who showed up — not a waste.

  

\> These aren’t throwaway days. With the right tools, they can be some of your best.
